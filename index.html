About

My name is Ethan Scott and I am an Industrial Engineer, currently studying at the University of Tennessee. I am 20 years old and living permanently in Nashville, Tennessee.

All of my interests have always been related to or directly involving systems or management, whether I've been aware of it or not. I am most interested in aviation because of the logical work flow of operating all of the aircraft systems juxtaposed with the simple enjoyment of travelling freely in the skies. I am also an avid sports fan, mostly because of my interest in finding the correct players and strategies to make the team operate at its maximum potential. Both of these interests are other applications of natural skills I have that I would be able to apply to any job: managing machines and changing settings of a process to ensure maximum efficiency. These principles have always been primary in my mind, and they solidify my love for Industrial Engineering.

Co-op

I am currently in between terms of a Co-op at Scepter Inc. in Waverly, Tennessee. Scepter is a secondary aluminum processing company headquartered in Waverly, its largest customers and associates being Alcoa and Novelis. Founded in 1986, it now has locations in New York, Indiana, Canada and Hungary.

I got this job through my father, who is the current company president after my grandfather, who is now the CEO. I felt that if I was being gifted a position co-op position that I had more work to do if I was to validly prove my worth to the company. In the Summer of 2018, I worked in mobile equipment maintenance and did other tasks around the plant, but my primary function was to perform an educated study on the plant's maintenance processes and try to solve the issues that were so prominent in this specific location. This involved extensive data gathering, organization and analysis into the many facets and sources of issues. While creating a report, I needed to make sure I managed my time effectively in creating systems by which to gather data while also using communication skills to get accounts directly from workers and supervisors around the plant. It was difficult to start out, but I soon found every day to be a nice, fulfilling challenge that ended in a very successful presentation to the company's executives.
